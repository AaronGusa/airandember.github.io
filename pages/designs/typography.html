Where words speak louder

Typography is the craft of arranging letters so language becomes both readable and expressive. Long before a visitor reads a single sentence, the type has already told them what kind of page this is. Explore the demos below to see — and feel — how that works.

About this design

Typography-driven design treats letterforms as the primary visual material: bold headers, delicate subheadings, deliberate spacing, and dynamic layouts carry the page with little or no imagery. Done well, it establishes hierarchy, sets tone, and guides the eye — all with the same tools printers have refined for five centuries. This page uses Air&Ember’s own local fonts, including a variable font (Playfair Display) whose weight you can stretch continuously in the playground below.

Demo 1 — The Type Playground

Every term below is a real typographer’s word. Tracking is the space between all letters; leading (rhymes with “sledding”) is the space between lines; and weight is the thickness of the strokes. Playfair Display is a variable font, so its weight axis is continuous — not just “regular or bold.” The text is editable: click it and type your own words.

Demo 2 — Font Pairing

Good pairings give the heading and the body different jobs: one provides voice, the other provides comfort. Bad pairings make two display faces compete for attention. Try each preset on the sample card.

The Kiln Runs Hot Tonight

Down in the valley workshop, the evening fire settles into a steady glow. Each piece on the shelf carries the mark of the hand that shaped it — a quiet argument for doing things slowly and well.

Why it works: a high-contrast serif display over a clean geometric sans — classic editorial contrast. Each face has a clear, separate job.

Demo 3 — Why Hierarchy Matters

Hierarchy is the size, weight, and spacing system that tells readers what to look at first. Flip it off and the same article becomes a wall of words — nothing invites the eye in, and skimming becomes impossible.
