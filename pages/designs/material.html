Material Web Design

Material Design treats the screen like sheets of physical paper and ink: surfaces sit at measurable heights, light casts believable shadows, and every touch responds with motion. It champions a modern, tactile aesthetic built on intuitive layers, subtle elevation, and bold-but-disciplined color, bridging art and usability so interfaces feel as natural as the physical world they borrow from.

ⓘ Every button and card on this page carries Material’s signature ink ripple — click or tap anything to watch the wave spread from your fingertip. The ripple confirms where you touched, not just that you touched.

Elevation Playground

Material expresses hierarchy through light. The higher a surface floats, the softer and wider its shadow grows.

ⓘ Drag the slider. Material defines elevation in dp (density-independent pixels): cards rest at 1–2dp, raised buttons at 6dp, navigation drawers at 16dp, dialogs at 24dp. Shadows aren’t decoration — they tell the user which surface is closest and therefore most important right now.

Cards at Rest

Cards are Material’s workhorse surface: a 1dp sheet that lifts to 8dp on hover, grouping related content into one touchable unit.

Built Structure

Surfaces snap to an 8dp grid — padding, gaps, and sizes are all multiples of eight, giving layouts quiet, dependable rhythm.

Natural Hierarchy

Type follows a strict scale — display, headline, title, body, label — so readers always know what matters most at a glance.

Layered Surfaces

Content nests like paper sheets: each layer earns its elevation, and shadows communicate the stacking order without a single border.
